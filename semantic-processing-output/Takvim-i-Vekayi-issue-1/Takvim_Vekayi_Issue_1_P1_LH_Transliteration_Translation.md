## Summary Translation (Flowing English)

**[Continuation from RH Column]**

...Previously, what was called the "dörice house" [would] provide provisions to all the inhabitants of the locality at the price given in times of abundance, so that they would fulfill this duty. Similarly, substantial amounts required were given to the inhabitants and subjects of the districts within the Çirmen Sanjak. [His Majesty] commanded [this], and they became recipients of [the people's] prayers.

In Gallipoli, however many mosques, fountains, and schools there were, their repair was ordered, along with the water channels whose conduits had been broken for a long time. Blessed sites were visited, especially in Bolayır, where Gazi Süleyman Pasha's tomb was visited, and alms were given to its tomb-keepers and to the poor.

The notables of Gallipoli wished to arrange a grand banquet at their own expense for the imperial retinue in Bolayır, but with the consideration that it might burden the common subjects, [His Majesty] ordered its abandonment. In this way, it was shown that the comfort of the poor was paramount in His Imperial consideration.

[His Majesty inspected] the Sultaniye Fortress, Kilid-ül-Bahr [Key of the Sea], Kum Kale [Sand Fortress], Sedd-ül-Bahr [Barrier of the Sea] fortresses, Nara Point, and in those environs—including the imperial bastions [built previously by His Majesty]—and the fortifications of Bozcaada's fortress. At Kilid-ül-Bahr Fortress, [His Majesty] ordered the firing of the large cannons—each firing four-kantar shots with thirty-six okka of gunpowder. The artillery chief and soldiers, although they aimed at the notch [target], due to the awe of the exalted assembly [the Sultan's presence], proper attention was not paid. On the sea's surface, in the first attempt, it was observed by His Majesty that the shots did not ricochet as expected. With imperial instruction regarding the aiming, when fired again, the shots ricocheted nine times more and penetrated about five arşun [approximately 3.5 meters] into the ground, devastating it.

All the fortress officers, imams, and servants received robes of honor and gifts, being gratified. The poor subjects also attained abundant alms.

[His Majesty] resided in the protected city of Edirne for ten days. During this period, the first Friday [prayer was performed] at Sultan Selim Khan II's [Selimiye Mosque], and the second Friday at the Üç Şerefeli [Three-Balconied Mosque], going with a procession and performing the prayers following the Second Imam Efendi. Some blessed sites were visited, and gifts were graciously bestowed upon the poor and dervishes.

For the investigation of conditions, [His Majesty visited] twice the Çirmen Governor Hüseyin Pasha's gardens situated at the confluence of the Maritsa and Tunca rivers, and once Tağdeviran-zade Kadri Bey's garden on the Tunca riverbank.

The day after entering Edirne, [His Majesty] honored the Mansure [Victorious] Soldiers' barracks—which is among his imperial works—viewing its four sides place by place, and entering its noble mosque. The edifice's style and solidity were approved. The soldiers present stood battalion by battalion, and passing through their midst, [His Majesty gained] personal awareness of each one's clothing, conditions, and states—with imperial endeavor making their officers recipients of favor and grace. That their supplies always be prepared was His Imperial desire, [this being conveyed] to Governor-General Ahmed Pasha, commander of the Special Division who came with the imperial retinue. There, openly, [His Majesty] ordered, showing kindness to the soldiers.

[His Majesty] also personally gained information about the hospital's conditions, ordering matters to ensure the continuation of order. The settlement of matters was entrusted to Ahmed Pasha and to the deputy chief physician—one of the former officials of Mecca the Honored, the Special Physician Abdülhak Efendi—with emphasis on healing and compassion. And [His Majesty] also ordered the hospital's repair and renewal.

On the Maritsa River, the great bridge that had collapsed from the flood current previously—its renewal was ordered. The people, coming and going by boats, were saved from the hardship they endured, and became devoted to praying for the continuation of His Majesty's state.

Each one of the servants who were felicitous with the honor of the imperial retinue, departing from the protected city of Edirne during the imperial movement, attained their share of gifts and joyfully offered prayers of beneficence for His Majesty. The exalted visit to the protected city of Edirne is hereby remembered and commemorated...
