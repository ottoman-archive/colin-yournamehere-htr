## Summary Translation (Flowing English)

### Section 1: The Beyoğlu Fire and Relief Efforts

On the twenty-third of Safar [August 3, 1831], a fire broke out by chance in the quarter named Çukur in Beyoğlu. Although the Acting Grand Vizier Pasha, the Commander-in-Chief Pasha, and other senior and junior officers and officials exerted effort and zeal from all sides to extinguish it, because of the severity of the wind, the fire extended for about fifteen hours, spreading through the districts of Dört Yol Ağzı, toward Taksim, Tatavla, and Aynalı Çeşme.

The homes and shops of Muslims, ambassadors and merchants of friendly states, and the Greek, Armenian, and Catholic communities were burned. His Majesty our Master the Padishah, foundation of compassion—whose imperial good intentions, subject-cherishing, and compassion-showing [are his characteristics]—as required by his nature, [ordered] that all the fire victims in Galata, Cihangir, the Capital, and Üsküdar be settled in the empty residences situated there, with their rents [covered].

An imperial decree of compassion was issued, and purely out of regard for the rights of his servants, a substantial imperial gift appeared. Whether from the Tophane and naval soldiers, from the shipyard workers, from other Muslims, or from the Greek, Armenian, or Catholic groups—to all those who had been burned out, [assistance was given]. Within one day, they were to be settled in the mentioned places.

It being the imperial desire that attention be paid to this command, the Commander-in-Chief notified the relevant officials. Immediately the Shipyard Steward, the Harbor Master, and the Admiral Pasha's Steward personally rose to action for settling the people's affairs. Besides their taking initiative, although the Commander-in-Chief was unwell, the Imperial Division Commander Ahmed Pasha, along with the Superintendent of Galata (who was the Fresh Fruit Customs Officer) and the Timber Superintendent Sarım Bey, went to Beyoğlu and personally handled the belongings, attending to the transport matter.

On the mentioned day, since shelter could not immediately be procured, some subjects with their children and families were placed at Kağıthane in stables designated for the Imperial Cavalry, after cleaning and arranging them. Since the mentioned subjects were exhausted, by the Commander-in-Chief Pasha's arrangement, an official was appointed from among the Imperial Army's officers to see to their necessary needs.

Besides showing various forms of compassion, by sublime will, from the Sublime Porte, flowers, sweets, and such gifts were sent to the ambassadors [of the affected friendly states], and with the dispatch of memoranda, the ceremony of condolence and hospitality was deemed worthy of imperial favor.

### Section 2: Catholic Church Permission in Galata

The greatest source of splendor for the entire state is subject-cherishing, and to this principle His Majesty constantly adheres. Among the subjects and people who are found to be loyal subordinates—those who visibly exercise care for the flourishing of sincerity and uprightness in their affairs—regarding the rights of communities and groups, His Majesty prefers to show traces of kindness and compassion, leading to a day-by-day increase in the beauty and splendor of the sultanate.

The auspicious-adorning, majestic Padishah, whose imperial commands are accompanied by execution—His Majesty our Master—according to his imperial will expressing compassion, the Catholic community's properties situated in Galata were ordered to be delegated to them. Salih Bey Efendi, the Deputy Superintendent of the Imperial Kitchen, was appointed to settle this matter. With the execution of the exchange conditions, especially in Galata, permission was granted for building a church. With this, the mentioned community was gladdened beyond what they could imagine.

### Section 3: The Shkodra Rebellion and Alexandria Siege

His Majesty our Master, the Sovereign renowned for justice—may his glory and eternal sultanate endure—in His Imperial Majesty's view, the viziers, ulema, and notables of the Sublime State, the Imperial Army, and the Victorious Army—in uprightness and in the essence of loyalty, there is absolutely no difference among them. And each of them, although incapable of expressing sufficient gratitude, is immersed in blessings.

Even so, now, in the imperial fortress of Alexandria [Egypt], there is [the rebel] besieged, whose capture and elimination is expected with divine eternal aid to happen soon—the Shkodran [Mustafa Pasha Bushati]. Several months ago, at Prelije, in the battle in that vicinity, His Excellency the Grand Vizier was personally present together with the forces...

## Historical Context Notes

1. **Beyoğlu Fire (23 Safar 1247 / August 3, 1831):** This was a major conflagration that affected the cosmopolitan Pera district, home to foreign embassies, merchants, and diverse religious communities. The relief effort demonstrates early Tanzimat-era emphasis on equal treatment of all subjects.

2. **Catholic Church Permission:** This represents a significant concession, as church construction was traditionally restricted. The delegation of Catholic properties and permission for a new church building reflects the reformist spirit of Mahmud II's reign.

3. **Shkodra Rebellion:** Mustafa Pasha Bushati (also known as Mustafa Reshid Pasha of Shkodra) was a powerful Albanian lord who resisted centralization. The reference to the Grand Vizier's personal involvement indicates the seriousness of this campaign.

4. **Alexandria Reference:** The mention of Alexandria likely refers to operations related to the Egyptian question or possibly confused with another fortress siege during this period.
