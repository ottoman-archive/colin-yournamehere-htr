## Summary Translation (Flowing English)

### Section 1: Military Honors for the Shkodra Campaign

[Continuing from previous column] With a brave assault, routing and scattering [the enemy] in a single attack, [the troops] demonstrated their particular loyalty. From the regiments of the Victorious Army—the second and third regiments' first and second battalions; the fourth regiment's second battalion; the seventh regiment; the eighth, thirteenth, fourteenth, fifteenth, and seventeenth regiments' first battalions—to all the senior and junior officers and enlisted men, according to their ranks and degrees, one medal of glory made of gold and silver titled the "Medal of Honor" (Nişân-ı İftihâr) and one patent of high glory was bestowed. With the sending of this imperial gift, each one—especially the Supreme Commander, the aforementioned His Excellency [the Grand Vizier], who was honored with a jeweled medal and thus became pointed at with admiration—[received recognition].

## Historical Context Notes

1. **Nişân-ı İftihâr (Medal of Honor):** This was one of the first modern Ottoman decorations, instituted by Mahmud II as part of his military and administrative reforms. The ceremony described here represents a major imperial investiture.

2. **Seyyid Mehmed Esad Efendi:** Listed here as "Takvîm-i Veḳâyi' Nâẓırı"—this is the founding editor of the very newspaper we are reading. His inclusion in the honors list is notable.

3. **Mustafa Behcet Efendi:** Serving as both Rumelia Kazasker (Chief Military Judge) and Hekimbaşı (Chief Physician), he represents the combination of religious and medical authority characteristic of Ottoman learned culture.

4. **Mustafa Reşid Bey (Âmedî):** Listed as the Reception Officer—this is likely the future Grand Vizier Mustafa Reşid Pasha, architect of the Tanzimat reforms, here appearing in an early bureaucratic post.

5. **Mehmed Sadık Rifat Bey:** Listed as son of the Tersâne Emîni—this is likely the future statesman Sadık Rifat Pasha, another key figure of the Tanzimat era.

6. **The Grand Vizier's Campaign:** The opening lines reference the Shkodra campaign where the Grand Vizier personally led forces against Mustafa Pasha Bushati, the rebel Albanian lord. The military units listed received the Medal of Honor for their role in that campaign.
