## Summary Translation (Flowing English)

### The Çamlık/Paramythia Rebellion

From the Çamlık group situated in Delvina district, a small [number] of their traitors whom they call "Brunio" broke the staff [of obedience] and registered themselves among the people of misery [rebels]. However, in the shadow of the imperial might, observing the assault of the Grand Vizier, they fled to Korka. And the others, henceforth giving hostages as guarantee not to leave obedience, became seekers of pardon and safety. Their hostages were settled as hostages in Yanya [Ioannina] fortress, and thus the Çamlık trouble was calmed.

Now, the mentioned fugitives, with the incitement of the Kefe [Shkodra] bandits, being [thus] allied, went out to the Paramythia district, inciting and seducing all of its populace. For the protection of Paramythia fortress, with promises and giving of money to the Albanian soldiers who had been previously stationed there by the Grand Vizier, they made them partners with themselves. They seized also several other fortresses. After taking them into their calamitous hand, they planned with an assault on Yanya to take the aforementioned hostages and, with the vain desire of removing the imperial army currently at Melbis, considerable numbers of bandits were coming.

This news [came] from the Yanya Deputy, who is from the honored commanders, the son of the Grand Vizier—Emin Pasha. When he received news, he took four companies from the third battalion in the Grand Vizier's retinue, and accompanying the other military units present in Yanya, he hastened to the village named Velcişte situated at several hours' distance. With the mentioned bandits, he showed manliness and endurance for about three hours. A considerable number of the mentioned bandits perished, and they fled to Lakoz Hor, Mar Aliç, Gorica, and Galbeki—that is, they fortified themselves in their fortresses.

In this interval, the Salonica Regiment was sent from the Grand Vizier's side to Emin Pasha's retinue. Pursuing the mentioned bandits, when he arrived upon them, understanding that they would not be able to withstand, they abandoned the mentioned fortresses and fled to the aforementioned Paramythia town, shutting themselves in its fortress. The necessary soldiers were placed and stationed at the mentioned fortresses, and the populace and subjects were secured and shown kindness. After this, the forces arrived at the mentioned town.

But since the mentioned bandits were insisting on rebellion, from Yanya fortress the necessary amount of cannons and mortars were brought. For about twenty-five days, night and day, the mentioned fortress was bombarded. The mentioned bandits, not being able to endure the fire of the cannons and bombs, with confession, abandoning their weapons, petitioned for pardon and safety to be given to their lives.

For this kind of penitents who show regret for their crimes, clinging to the skirts of mercy, the execution of the treatment of pardon in their case conforms to the mercy-containing pleasure of His Imperial Majesty, which is his choice motto. Since this is so, their petitions were granted, and in that manner, the fortress was seized.

According to the emerging opinion of the Grand Vizier, the source of corruption and instigator of the obstinate people—the twelve Brunio traitors—were sent for now to the Monastir side. At the end of Rebiülevvel [~August 1831], the entire Çamlık group, showing obedience, thus its trouble was eliminated.

Observing the rule of prudence and caution, from now on, ensuring corruption would not occur from among themselves, about thirty of their esteemed notables were taken as hostages, and their sending to Salonica fortress was approved and arranged. In these days, they are being sent. And among them, being involved in the Paramythia tumult, in that battle eighteen individuals were captured—to be bound and placed to the galley as oarsmen—and were sent with the Chief Courier Ismail Agha to the Sublime State [Istanbul]. This is recorded in the mentioned Pasha's dispatches presented to the Sublime Porte.

The mentioned persons were placed to the galley. The mentioned Agha was given the finest parasol-holder, and to the two couriers coming in his retinue, plain finest coats were bestowed, and an imperial gift was given. The mentioned Pasha—truly, a lion's cub—is worthy of his description. With the will expressing favor of His Imperial Majesty, containing praise and "Bravo!"—from the side of the Acting Grand Vizier—with the dispatching of a sublime letter, kindness was bestowed.

### Appointment: Ada-i Kebir Guardianship

On the Vidin side, the guardianship of the Great Island [Ada-i Kebir], which was in the responsibility of the Bosnian Süleyman Paşazades' eldest—from the commanders, Ibrahim Pasha—having been ill for some time, this time with the arrival of his promised death [appointed hour], he died. This was reported.

That family has been from the loyal servants of the Sublime State from before. Since this is so, the mentioned guardianship was appointed to the mentioned deceased's middle brother, Cemaleddin Bey, with the rank of commander (mir-i miran).

## Historical Context Notes

1. **Çamlık Rebellion:** The Çamlık (Tsamouria in Greek) region was part of the broader Albanian resistance to Ottoman centralization in the early 1830s. The "Kefe eşḳıyâsı" (Shkodra bandits) refers to the forces of Mustafa Pasha Bushati, whose rebellion was the major military operation of this period.

2. **Emin Pasha:** Described as the son of the Grand Vizier (Serdâr-ı Ekrem), serving as Yanya Kaymakamı. His successful suppression of the Paramythia rebellion earned him the title "şibl-i esed" (lion's cub) and imperial praise.

3. **Paramythia:** A strategic town in Epirus (now Greece), its fortress was besieged for 25 days with artillery brought from Ioannina before the rebels surrendered.

4. **Küreğe vażʿ (Galley Sentencing):** The 18 captured rebels were sentenced to serve as galley oarsmen—a traditional Ottoman punishment for criminals and rebels.

5. **Hostage System:** The taking of 30 hostages from the Çamlık notables to Salonica represents standard Ottoman practice for ensuring compliance from recently-subdued populations.

6. **Bosna'lı Süleyman Paşazade Family:** A loyal notable family from Bosnia holding the Ada-i Kebir guardianship on the Danube (near Vidin). The succession from Ibrahim Pasha to his brother Cemaleddin Bey demonstrates the hereditary nature of such positions among loyal provincial families.

7. **Ada-i Kebir (Great Island):** A strategic Danube island near Vidin, important for controlling river traffic and defending against potential threats from the north.
