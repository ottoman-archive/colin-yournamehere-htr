## Summary Translation (Flowing English)

As an occasion for commemoration, coins of various denominations—the ḫayriye, fetḥiye, and quarter pieces—were struck bearing the name and inscription of the town [visited], for blessing. These were distributed to all the servants present in the imperial procession. After the imperial return, the ulema and notables who were present at the imperial reception were honored with this precious gift.

As at the other stages [of the journey], in Edirne the Sharia Judge Seyyid Ahmed Efendi and all the ulema, sheikhs, notables, and dignitaries from the surrounding districts came to see the imperial procession with utmost eagerness. The notables who came to visit were honored in the imperial presence according to their ranks with the finest wool ferâce and broadcloth kukuleta robes of honor. Besides this, the priests of the three communities [Greek, Armenian, Jewish] and rabbis, along with the community heads and elders with them, were given appropriate robes of honor.

The Muslim children with their teachers, and the children of the three communities and their teachers—all the Muslim and dhimmi poor were registered and gladdened with gifts and abundant alms. Especially, some blessed tombs were visited, and the sheikhs and dervishes were given the equivalent of votive offerings and other gifts.

On the eighteenth of Muharram [1247], which was a Friday [corresponding to late June 1831], after performing the Friday prayer at the eighth hour, His Majesty departed from Edirne. Out of complete compassion for the people of Istanbul, he traveled with swift journey. On the twenty-first, a Sunday—the next day—His Majesty arrived at the Silivri stage.

On the imperial return from Edirne, the first stage was Havsa—that is, the town of Havass Mahmud Pasha. The second stage was Burgaz. The third stage was Çorlu; the fourth stage was Silivri. The ulema and notables of Edirne, the mentioned children, and all the people and other communities participated in the imperial send-off as they had in the reception, fittingly raising prayers for the sovereign's wellbeing to the heavens.

The judges, notables, and leading persons of the mentioned stages fulfilled their obligation of send-off and reception, coming from places half an hour away, walking and gratefully performing their duties, thereby acquiring the honor of robes of honor and attaining gifts and favor. All the Muslims and subjects of each place—the poor were registered and received the most abundant alms.

At places on the passage route of the imperial procession, such as in the town of Baba-yı Atik, an imperial decree appeared for the renewal and repair of the mosque, school, and fountain situated there, as well as a mosque in the village of Kanklı that had become a ruined church. Likewise, the poor of Uzun and the inhabitants of Karışdıran village were gladdened with imperial gifts.

Especially, on the lower road, when His Imperial Majesty observed that some fields' harvest had not yet ripened and was still unharvested, he commanded provisions to be procured for the poor villages, issuing a direct command to Hüseyin Pasha, the Governor of Sofia. The tranquility of the conditions of the poor was His Majesty's sincere sovereign concern, as this subject-cherishing imperial treatment also demonstrated.

In Silivri, by His Majesty's will, the previously prepared imperial steamship [was boarded]. The Admiral of the Sea Halil Pasha, who was in the imperial procession, and the Secretary of Secrets Mustafa Efendi, and the Admiral of the Imperial Shipyard Ahmed Pasha, and Chamberlain Ahmed Bey and their excellencies, and the other chamberlains who were present—on Tuesday, His Majesty honored them by departing at the second hour. Around the seventh hour, they arrived and cast anchor before the village of Ayastefanos [San Stefano]. In the mentioned village, when His Imperial Majesty honored the Çamlıca lower fortress with a visit, he bestowed his grace by arriving at the pavilion that had been specially built for him as a resting place.

Since departing from Gallipoli, Hüseyin Pasha had been in the imperial retinue performing his duty of service and acquiring various favors. The aforementioned Hüseyin Pasha, who took pride in his service, had his service accepted by His Imperial Majesty, and he was given permission to return from Silivri.

The imperial return of His Imperial Majesty was now certain. The pillars of the Sublime Sultanate, who were generally awaiting the sovereign's arrival, began undertaking the ceremony of grateful reception. Meanwhile, the complete expectation and longing of the Victorious Muhammadan Army was also in effect. His Excellency Mehmed Hüsrev Pasha, the Commander-in-Chief of the Victorious Muhammadan Army, presented and expressed this to the imperial presence through his intermediation.

Since it was the time of summer heat, out of the utmost royal compassion, the others were to remain in their places to offer prayers, acclamations, and fire salutes. Only each regiment's first and second battalions were commanded to be prepared for the reception. In that manner, they were brought out, with two companies of Imperial Cavalry at the head on the right and two companies of artillery soldiers at the head on the left.

Three days before the imperial arrival, on the vast plain in front of the imperial palace in the aforementioned village [Ayastefanos], the Commander-in-Chief, through his expertise, arranged and set up the troops in the formation called the "attack column." The troops were prepared in their tents, and upon the imperial arrival, the victorious soldiers—may the army last with divine confirmations—emerged from their tents in attack column formation, column by battalion...
