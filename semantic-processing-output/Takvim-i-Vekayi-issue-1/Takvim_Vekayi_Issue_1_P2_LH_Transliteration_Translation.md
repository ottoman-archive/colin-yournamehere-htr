## Summary Translation (Flowing English)

After [the military review], at the salute cannons, His Majesty our Master reviewed the soldiers. Coming from the right flank, in order to enter the mentioned palace facing toward the qibla, the decoration of the gate on that side for the auspicious moment was arranged by the aforementioned Marshal through his judgment and arrangement—using colorful gold-threaded fabric and new designs of military implements with the crescent and star emblem, and inscribed red flags. The aforementioned Marshal had completely devoted himself to the station of service to the Padishah.

On the day of the imperial arrival, His Excellency the Şeyhülislam es-Seyyid Abdülvehhab Efendi and the Acting Grand Vizier (Ḳâ'immaḳâm-ı Rikâb-ı Hümâyûn) Hulusi Ahmed Pasha, and as had been arranged, the great ulema, renowned notables, and other servants came one after another and rested in the tents that had been erected.

The next day, Wednesday, after partaking of the foods prepared by the Acting Grand Vizier, the Şeyhülislam and the other ulema stood to the left of the palace's mounting stone; the viziers and notables stood outside the decorated gate; and the soldiers, in a heart-captivating arrangement, lined the royal road of reception where they waited.

His Majesty the Padishah, Refuge of Islam—may his days of sultanate be honored, blessed with fortune and adorned with the lights of blessings until the promised Day of Judgment—our Master, from the previously mentioned pavilion, mounted the equipped horse and reviewed the soldiers' columns with a gaze of gracious salute, bestowing upon them joy. When the elite soldiers came under imperial observation, rank by rank they shouted "My Padishah, long live!" raising their prayers and acclamations.

Upon His Majesty's approach to the mentioned gate, the viziers and notables, with bent stature, received the salute and went forward several steps, preceding the imperial horse. They achieved twin victory. Upon His Majesty's entry to the palace square, the notables and the regimental commanders running at the imperial stirrup lined up on the right side according to their ranks.

His Majesty the Sovereign of chosen conduct, our Master, gladdened all those waiting with the favor of his salute. With the Acting Grand Vizier and the Admiral Pasha being his lords, His Majesty honored the upper floor of the palace. The Şeyhülislam, the viziers, the ulema up to the rank of İstanbul Kadısı, and the notables up to the Superintendent of the Imperial Kitchen were given entry to the imperial presence and permission to kiss His Majesty's foot and the hem of his imperial robes—thus honored with kindness.

After about one hour of rest, again the prayer-sayers standing at the station of salute, the servants, and the soldiers' columns became recipients of His Majesty's gracious gaze. From the threshold side of the mentioned palace, His Majesty exited through the gate and boarded the imperial steamship. Upon His Majesty's arrival and upon his imperial return from the mentioned palace, the batteries at that place and the soldiers of famed fidelity, being at the left head, and the twelve pieces of artillery drawn up before the battalions—with the execution and firing of the fire salute ceremony, the ceremony of joy was performed.

The inhabitants of the Capital of the Sublime Sultanate, out of their complete longing—on the Istanbul side, from Yedikule to the Çırağan Waterfront Palace, and on the Üsküdar side, from Kadıköy to a point opposite the mentioned palace—big and small, male and female, crowded the piers and towers. The observation towers and boats were completely full.

Especially in the Sublime Threshold and the three lands [Galata, Üsküdar, Eyüp], the school children with their teachers were in boats and on the shores. At Yenikapı were the Armenians; at Kumkapı, the Greek community; at Ahırkapı, the Jews; and at Kabataş, the Catholic group's patriarch and clergy. The rabbis and altogether the school children had put on their garments of reverence and had been awaiting the imperial arrival.

The World-Protecting Padishah, our Master His Majesty, as he cast the light of his beauty upon those waiting from his imperial steamship, they raised the heartfelt sound of prayers and amens. From Bâlâ and Topkapı and Tophane and the other fortresses, and from the French ambassador's ship that was present in the Golden Horn at this time, cannons were fired in the style of joy. From Davud Paşa and Rami and the Gate of the Commander-in-Chief and Selimiye and the other imperial barracks, the sounds of the fire salutes of the soldiers manifesting grace adorned the sky.

His Majesty our Master the Padishah of Lofty Titles, with this constellation of joy, on the thirty-third day of his departure from the Sublime Threshold—that is, this blessed year forty-seven [1247 AH], Muharram's twenty-fourth and June's twenty-third [discrepancy in original], occurring on a Tuesday—with fortune and prosperity, at around the eighth hour, honored the Çırağan Waterfront Palace with his presence.

May this journey of victorious traces be a prelude to various blessings, and may His Majesty's life and majesty be companion to blessings until the end of time, amen.

For the Muslims situated in the Sublime Threshold and the three lands, His Majesty gave fifty thousand kuruş to their schools; to the Greek schools, twenty thousand; to the Armenian and Jewish schools, five thousand each; and to the Catholic group, five thousand kuruş—this sublime imperial gift appeared. All of them were distributed through the officials' administration, and all rose to persevere in prayer for the permanence of His Majesty's life and imperial majesty.
