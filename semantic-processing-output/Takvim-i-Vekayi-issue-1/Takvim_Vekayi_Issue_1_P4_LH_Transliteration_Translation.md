## Summary Translation (Flowing English)

### The Ceremony Attendees (Continued from Previous Column)

[Continued from P4 RH] ...the First and Second Imams of His Imperial Majesty our Master; and from the commanders, the Imperial Division Commander and the Artillery Chief; and the Imperial Palace Tutors. To the right side of the imperial throne [stood] the Şeyhülislam Efendi His Excellency, both dismissed and appointed senior ulema, the Chief of the Prophet's Descendants (Nakibüleşraf), and the ulema, notables, superintendents, and servants of the Sublime Porte at the Istanbul rank.

Facing the imperial throne [were] the Imperial regimental commanders (miralay) and their lords, the Victorious Army, Arsenal, and Bomb Works regimental commanders, and the deputy and regiment superintendents. Following them, the Aghas of the Imperial Stirrup, and at this time, the thirty persons designated as Chief Gatekeeper Aghas—toward the imperial palace, in an arc-shaped formation, wearing council garments—that is, turbaned, and official cloaks; and the viziers, notables, and others wore gold-braided jackets, swords, and plain fezzes—all lined up.

The Commander-in-Chief Pasha His Excellency, in this most sacred assembly, completely stood in the station of service.

### The Sultan's Arrival

His Majesty our majesty-stationed Master, only with the intimates of the sultanate and the feast-tutor and Imperial servants, informally, wearing a jeweled fez and Albanian cloak, walking from the imperial palace, honored them with his appearance and gladdened the onlookers with his gaze of favor, commanding. After His Majesty illuminated their sublime throne, with imperial permission, the prince His Excellency—with jeweled Quran purses and lectern and cushions, their tutors and other officials being equal in their reverence—only the regimental commanders of the Imperial and Victorious Army, the steward, the Chief Treasurer Bey Efendi, the Chief of Scribes Efendi, the Signature Keeper, the Imperial Endowments, Revenue, and Expenditures Superintendents—the efendis in front of them; and with the Chief of the Abode of Felicity and the Treasurer Agha, their excellencies, supporting him under his arms—the prince was brought from the imperial palace.

### The Bed'-i 'Ulûm Ceremony

Upon his entry to the imperial tent, the prince rubbed his face to the imperial foot [kissed the Sultan's foot] and stood in the imperial presence. At that moment, to the right side of the imperial throne, on the spread white ihram, the Şeyhülislam Efendi took his place, and the jeweled lectern was placed. And to their cushions also, the prince of high lineage His Excellency sat. After attending to reading, the Şeyhülislam Efendi rose to his feet and offered prayer. And with his gesture, from their tents, the mentioned children also offered the supplication of "Amen."

At the conclusion of the prayer, the prince His Excellency again approached the imperial dust-foot [kissed the Sultan's foot], and again in their resting place, stood with hands folded. Up to the Chief Gatekeeper Aghas, altogether the attendees, after being honored with the imperial dust-kissing, stood again in their places. The prince His Excellency, with sublime permission, according to the ceremony by which they came, was returned to the imperial palace. That is, following this, His Imperial Majesty our Master returned from their imperial court to the imperial palace.

### The Imperial Feast

Afterwards, the viziers, ulema, notables, officers, and aghas—in their designated tents, they were honored with exquisite foods from the imperial kitchen, according to rank. And all the enlisted soldiers and school children were gladdened with an imperial gift, and served sweetened pilaf with saffron rice (zerde). Even the female spectators and listeners present at Haydarpaşa—uncountable—and uncountable males and females—all became heart-satisfied from the boundless imperial table.

After the meal, the attendees were given permission to return to their places. His Majesty our majesty-stationed Master, in that joy-increasing space, until Saturday [the day after Friday], rested with their Imperial soldiers.

### Editorial Note: Innovation Beyond Tradition

At the princes' excellencies' beginning of reading, from the ulema, only the Şeyhülislam Efendi His Excellency, and actually only the Rumelia and Anatolia Chief Military Judges, along with the Chief of the Prophet's Descendants efendis were traditionally present, while others were not traditionally present—this was the ancient custom.

But our Padishah our Master His Majesty, purely from his great disposition of world-pleasing fortune and the tradition of prayer-cherishing nature of the imperial majesty, arising from this, altogether invited the senior ulema and the prayer-sayers at the Istanbul rank, with the kindness of his invitation—thus from this aspect also, His Majesty commanded respect for the glory of noble knowledge.

### The Military Demonstration

As a requirement of the imperial will, in this sublime place, in the context of stimulating the eagerness of the soldiers, from the regiments present in the Abode of Felicity [Istanbul]—the first, seventh, ninth, tenth, and twelfth regiments—one battalion each, totaling five battalions, were brought to the mentioned field.

On the tenth of Rebiülâhir [September 18, 1831], Saturday, they were transported. And their tents, at Haydarpaşa, in Ibrahim Ağa Meadow, situated—going toward the imperial palace, on the hill on the right side—in attack column order was set up, and in that manner, the battalions were encamped.

On Friday, imperial permission was issued for the battalions' return. Upon imperial instruction, the Commander-in-Chief Pasha, through his training, based on the order occurring from his side, with their arrangement, the assembly signal was struck. To march by the nearest road, the battalions, from the tents, in attack column formation, exiting by number from the hill, were descended down. After making a change of direction to the right, first, the first regiment's battalion—when it came to the level of the imperial palace, at that moment, gave a salute to the right and to the left.

## Historical Context Notes

1. **Bed'-i 'Ulûm ("Beginning of Learning") Ceremony:** This was the traditional Ottoman ceremony marking a prince's formal entry into education, typically involving recitation from the Quran and prayer by the Şeyhülislam. For 8-year-old Abdülmecid, this marked his training as heir apparent.

2. **Sultan's Informal Attire:** Mahmud II wearing a "murṣa' fes ve ḥırvânî" (jeweled fez and Albanian cloak) rather than full imperial regalia demonstrates the reform-era informality he promoted. The fez itself was a Mahmud II innovation, replacing the traditional turban.

3. **Expansion of Ceremony Attendees:** The editorial explicitly notes that traditionally only the Şeyhülislam, two Kazaskers, and Nakibüleşraf attended the bed'-i 'ulûm. Mahmud II expanded this to include all senior ulema and Istanbul-rank officials—presented as honoring "the glory of noble knowledge."

4. **Female Spectators:** The explicit mention of "ṣâḥibe ve semî'e" (female spectators and listeners) and "inâs̱" (females) among the crowds at Haydarpaşa is notable. They were fed from the "boundless imperial table."

5. **Military Demonstration:** The ceremony was combined with a military review, with five battalions from different regiments encamped and performing maneuvers—demonstrating the integration of military reform (the new Manṣûre army) with dynastic ceremonial.

6. **Zerde (Saffron Rice):** The feeding of soldiers and school children with sweetened pilaf and zerde (saffron rice dessert) was a traditional Ottoman celebration food, here distributed on an imperial scale.
